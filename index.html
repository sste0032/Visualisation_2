Analysis of Conflicts Across the Globe

This dashboard aims to analyse the inter and intra-state conflicts around the world from 1946 to 2022.

The data used was sourced from the Uppsala Conflict Data Program and the International Peace Research Institute, Olso.

Throughout this dashboard, we will focus on analysing the number of conflicts with regards to several geographical and temporal attributes.

Note: For the region attribute, 'Multiple' is used to indicate conflicts which involve governments/organisations from multiple regions.

To the right, we will analyse temporal trends and patterns. Firstly, we analyse the trends in the number of conflicts over time, also taking a deep dive into the regional trends. Secondly, we analyse the distribution of the conflicts' starting month across regions.

Across the bottom, the focus is on geographical analyses. Firstly, we analyse the number of conflicts across countries. Secondly, we analyse the pairs of organisations/governments which are often in conflict with each other. Lastly, we analyse the number of conflicts and their intensity across regions.

Note: Hover over charts to see extra information.

The overall number of conflicts has been increasing over time.

There was a peak in the early 1990s, mainly due to the Americas and Europe.

Note: To view a single region, select in the Region Selection drop down menu.

However, the maximum number of conflicts occurred in 2019, mainly fueled by Africa and the Middle East.

In the majority of regions, the most common month to start conflicts is December. Only the Middle East is different, with July being the most common.

Asia and Africa have the most conflicts overall.

Myanmar and India as the countries with the highest number of conflicts.

There are several countries with no conflicts, mainly located in Northern Europe and some in the Americas.

The most conflict heavy regions are the Middle East and Asia.

The Americas and Europe have the least number of conflicts, with no country above 12 conflicts.

There are no pairs of organisations/government with 3 or more conflicts in different regions.

The majority are between a government and organisation, except for the Egypt and Israel conflicts.
