Analysis of Conflicts Across the Globe

This dashboard aims to analyse the inter and intra-state conflicts around the world from 1946 to 2022.

The data used was sourced from the Uppsala Conflict Data Program and the International Peace Research Institute, Olso

To the right, we will analyse trends of the number of conflicts across years and the distribution of starting months of these conflicts.

At the bottom, the countries, sides (i.e. organisation or government) and regions involved in the conflicts are analysed.

Note: 'Multiple' is used to indicate conflicts which involve governments/organisations from multiple regions.

The overall number of conflicts has been increasing over time.

There was a peak in the early 1990s, mainly due to the Americas and Europe.

However, the maximum number of conflicts occurred in 2019, mainly fueled by Africa and the Middle East.

In the majority of regions, the most common month to start conflicts is December.

Only the Middle East is different, with July being the most common.

Myanmar and India as the countries with the highest number of conflicts.

The most conflict heavy regions are the Middle East and Asia.

All the conflicts are within the regions.

The majority are between a government and organisation, except for the Egypt and Israel conflicts.

Asia has the highest number of conflicts, followed by Africa and the Middle East.

The order is the same for the different intensities, however, Africa does have a greater proportion classed as War than Asia.
